Post 1: A Strange Identity from Fourier Land

July 18, 2025

Consider the curious identity: \[ \sum_{n = -\infty}^{\infty} \frac{1}{1 + (n - x)^2} = \pi \cdot \frac{\sinh(\pi)}{\cosh(\pi) - \cos(2\pi x)} \] which emerges from applying the Poisson summation formula to a Lorentzian. It’s surprising, tight, and deeply analytic. But why does it smell so much like complex analysis?
